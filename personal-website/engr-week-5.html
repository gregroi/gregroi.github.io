Engineering Week 5 Project Reflection

Due: 10/27/2023

TThis was a pretty cool project. It was simple and intuitive but still provided a challenge due to my time constraints. As opposed to other students in the class, I was absent for the first class and spent most of the second class trying to get TeamViewer and the class laptops to work. Therefore, I only had the third class to work on the project, though it only took me two iterations to complete the project.

For my first iteration, I used my box that I made by following the tutorial and changed the parameters so that the width and length fit the same dimensions of the alarm clock. I also reused the alarm clock cover from Project 4 in order to make a laser cut of it. Following these steps made it very easy to make a new box from laser cut wood. I projected the components that I wanted and exported them into DXF files, which allowed me to put them into Lightburn. I configured the different files into one LBRN file and then gave it to Ian to laser cut. I constructed it and it fit well, but was too small for the circuit board.

Between the two iterations, I practiced using Lightburn by making the laser-cut image of the Golden Gate Bridge. I found an image online and copy-pasted it onto the app, which gave me my Lightburn file. This just proved to me that Lightburn was an easier application than Fusion360 or any of the slicing programs that we used.

For the second iteration, I just changed the dimensions by 110%, which made the box bigger while allowing for the alarm clock to be visible. It was all done on the same LBRN file, so it took 10 minutes for this to be cut and then put together. I put it together and it ended up being solid! Super duper simple.

I prefer laser cutting and other subtractive manufacturing to additive manufacturing. Itâ€™s so much more intuitive for me and it takes a lot less time as a whole. Because of this benefit, I feel that the pros outway the cons making it the superior manufacturing method. It also seems to be that, though the layers are thicker, you can make different types of objects, especially through other reductive techniques, like sanding and water cutting.
